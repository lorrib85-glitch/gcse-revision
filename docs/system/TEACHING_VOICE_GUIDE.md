# Teaching Voice Guide

Tone of voice and writing-style rules for all learner-facing content
(module copy, screen text, narration, examiner feedback, etc.). Read this
before authoring or editing any content.

## Core principle

Every word costs attention. Every sentence must earn its place.

Write to communicate, never to impress.

## Goals

- Maximise understanding.
- Maximise memorability.
- Minimise cognitive load.
- Respect the learner's intelligence.

## Writing principles

- Every sentence must earn its place.
- Every paragraph should contain a worthwhile idea.
- Every screen should have a single purpose.
- Prefer clarity over elegance.
- Prefer precision over drama.
- Prefer concrete over abstract.
- Delete anything that doesn't improve understanding.

## Reading level

- Approximately age 11-13 readability.
- Short sentences.
- Short paragraphs.
- One idea at a time.
- Explain difficult vocabulary immediately.
- Never simplify by becoming inaccurate.

## Emotional tone

- Warm.
- Curious.
- Intelligent.
- Calmly confident.
- Never patronising.
- Never cheesy.
- Never over-excited.
- Never sound like marketing copy.

## Respect

- Never portray historical people as stupid.
- Explain why beliefs made sense at the time.
- Focus on available evidence, not hindsight.

## Curiosity

- Create questions before answers where appropriate.
- Reward curiosity quickly.
- Every chapter should contain at least one unforgettable insight.

## Stories

- Prefer people, choices, mistakes, discoveries and consequences.
- Use stories to teach, not to entertain.

## Interesting details

- Include vivid details only when they reinforce understanding.
- Remove trivia without educational value.

## Humour

- Rare.
- Dry.
- Natural.
- Never forced.
- Never meme-like.

## Praise

- Avoid empty encouragement.
- Confidence should come from understanding.

## Avoid AI-style writing

- No unnecessary em dashes.
- No "It's not X, it's Y" rhetoric.
- No "This serves as a powerful reminder".
- No "This highlights the importance of".
- No "It's worth noting that".
- No "Interestingly".
- No "As we can see".
- No "Let's dive in".
- No "Imagine you're".
- No fake significance.
- No floral language.
- No motivational filler.
- Prefer plain English.

## Final AI smell check

- Remove filler.
- Remove flourish.
- Remove inflated vocabulary.
- Delete sentences that exist only to sound impressive.
- Ask: would an outstanding human teacher naturally say this?

## Ultimate objective

Don't tell students what to think. Give them enough understanding that they
can't help thinking it.
