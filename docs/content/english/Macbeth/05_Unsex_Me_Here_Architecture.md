## 2. Architecture checklist (tailored)

### Section 1 — Hook & Prior Recall
- **Purpose:** Surface existing knowledge; prime retrieval.
- **Proposed content:**
  - Retrieve from Episode 4: What is the concealment motif? What does "Stars, hide your fires" mean?
  - Retrieve from Episode 3: What is Macbeth's aside? What does it reveal about his pre-existing ambition?
  - Hook: "Who do you think is more ambitious — Macbeth or Lady Macbeth?" (sets up the episode's question without pre-empting it)
  - True/false: "Lady Macbeth is the villain of Macbeth" / "Shakespeare presents Lady Macbeth as if she is calling on supernatural spirits" / "Lady Macbeth doubts whether she can carry out the murder"
- **Suggested components:**
  - `ChapterHookScreen` — true/false targeting Lady Macbeth complexity misconceptions
  - `PriorKnowledgeRecall` — Macbeth's "Stars, hide your fires"; concealment motif

### Section 2 — Plot & Dramatic Action
- **Purpose:** Teach what happens in the scene.
- **Proposed content:**
  - Lady Macbeth reads Macbeth's letter about the witches and prophecies
  - Her assessment of Macbeth: "too full o' the milk of human kindness"
  - Her first soliloquy: she positions herself as the one who must supply what Macbeth lacks
  - Her second soliloquy: "Come, you spirits" — the invocation to demonic powers
  - Macbeth arrives; she immediately takes control with the serpent/flower strategy
  - A messenger announces Duncan's imminent visit; her excitement and planning
- **Suggested components:**
  - `VisualLearning` — cinematic sequence: reading the letter → first soliloquy → invocation → Macbeth's arrival
  - `ExplainReveal` — why Lady Macbeth believes she needs to invoke spirits (Macbeth's kindness is the obstacle; she must suppress her own conscience)

### Section 3 — Theme & Context
- **Purpose:** Connect to major themes and AO3 context.
- **Proposed content:**
  - Theme: Gender — Lady Macbeth actively rejects Jacobean feminine expectations; "unsex me here" is a deliberate transgression
  - Theme: Ambition — Lady Macbeth's ambition is immediate, unimpeded; contrast with Macbeth's moral hesitancy
  - Theme: Supernatural — her invocation to spirits; for a Jacobean audience shaped by the 1604 Witchcraft Act and King James's interest in witchcraft, Lady Macbeth's invocation would feel spiritually dangerous and transgressive
  - Theme: Power — she is the dominant partner at this point; this is the high-water mark of her power
  - Theme: Concealment — Macbeth asks darkness to hide his desires; Lady Macbeth asks darkness and spirits to remake her body and conscience. Shakespeare makes them independently choose concealment before they plan together
  - Context: Jacobean gender expectations — women as passive, domestic, obedient; Lady Macbeth inverts all of these
  - Context: Demonic possession — Jacobean audiences may have read calling on spirits to enter one's body as a spiritually dangerous act
  - Context: Gunpowder Plot medal connection — "serpent under the flower" was the image on the commemorative medal
- **Suggested components:**
  - `TheoryCompareBlock` — Jacobean ideal of femininity vs Lady Macbeth's behaviour in this scene
  - `SwipeSort` — "feminine" (Jacobean expectation) vs "transgressive" — sort statements about Lady Macbeth
  - `ExplainReveal` — the Gunpowder Plot medal: serpent under flowers → Lady Macbeth instructs Macbeth to embody this exact image

### Section 4 — Quote Analysis (AO2)
- **Purpose:** Active retrieval and analysis.
- **Proposed content:**
  - **"too full o' the milk of human kindness"**: AO2: "full" = excess; "milk" = nurture/maternal; the adjective "human" makes kindness sound like a species characteristic being called a weakness; whole-play link: milk imagery returns in the invocation
  - **"Come, you spirits / That tend on mortal thoughts, unsex me here"**: AO2: imperatives ("Come", "unsex"); "mortal thoughts" = murderous impulses; "unsex" = strip away Jacobean feminine qualities; technique: invocation as a genre (prayer/ritual subverted). For a Jacobean audience shaped by the 1604 Witchcraft Act and King James's interest in witchcraft, this invocation would feel spiritually dangerous and transgressive
  - **"Take my milk for gall"**: AO2: milk/gall contrast; the inversion of maternal function; "gall" = bile/poison; whole-play link: the milk she wants replaced with poison resurfaces as guilt in 5.1
  - **"Look like the innocent flower, but be the serpent under't"**: AO2: serpent = biblical temptation; Gunpowder Plot medal reference; the appearance/reality strategy in one image
  - **"my dearest partner of greatness"**: AO2: the affectionate term; Macbeth writes to her as an equal; establishes the partnership before it breaks down
  - PQAC: students analyse "Come, you spirits / That tend on mortal thoughts, unsex me here" — full analysis
- **Suggested components:**
  - `MatchingTask` — match quote to theme to technique to whole-play link (5.1 reversal)
  - `FillInTheBlanksBlock` — complete PQAC for "unsex me here" and "take my milk for gall"
  - `ColSortBlock` — sort Lady Macbeth's qualities: strength / transgression / suppressed humanity

### Section 5 — Retrieval & Wider Play Links
- **Purpose:** Interleave; build whole-play awareness before the final exam-prep section.
- **Proposed content:**
  - Retrieve from Episode 4: What is the concealment motif? How does "Look like the innocent flower" extend "Stars, hide your fires"?
  - Retrieve from Episode 3: What was Macbeth's reaction to the prophecies? How does Lady Macbeth's reaction in her letter-reading soliloquy differ?
  - Whole-play link: "Take my milk for gall" in 1.5 ↔ "the smell of the blood still" in 5.1 — the suppression fails
  - Whole-play link: Macbeth asks darkness to hide his desires; Lady Macbeth asks darkness and spirits to remake her body and conscience. Shakespeare makes them independently choose concealment before they plan together
  - Forward link: How will Lady Macbeth's strategy ("Leave all the rest to me") play out in Episode 6?
- **Suggested components:**
  - `QuickRecallScreen` — 5 rapid-fire questions (high retrieval priority episode)
  - `RecoveryQuizPlayer` — targeted recovery

### Section 6 — Exam Practice
- **Purpose:** Final AQA-style practice moment. The final navigation/progress dot must land on exam prep. This is the highest-value episode for Lady Macbeth questions.
- **Proposed content:**
  - Question: "How far does Shakespeare present Lady Macbeth as a strong female character?" (AQA Jun 2024 actual question)
  - Key argument: Lady Macbeth's strength in 1.5 is genuine but requires the suppression of her natural conscience through supernatural language and self-transformation — this is not straightforward or secure strength
  - Model response plan (in 1.5 and across the play):
    1. Extract/whole-play link: she is strong — imperative language, clear planning, immediate resolve
    2. Extract/whole-play link: her strength requires spiritual and psychological self-suppression — she cannot be strong without attempting to remove compassion
    3. Whole play: her strength erodes from Act 3 (excluded from Banquo plot) to Act 5 (sleepwalking)
  - AQA Jun 2024 indicative content referenced: "ideas about her determination to murder Duncan — even before Macbeth returns"; "ideas about gender expectations"; "references to Lady Macbeth's calling on spirits"
- **Suggested components:**
  - `GuidedExamResponse` — 3-step essay plan: strength in the extract → limits of that strength → whole-play arc
  - `FaceTheExaminer` — students write their own paragraph independently, then see model response

## 4. Build recommendations

1. **Storyline integration:** The core takeaway (Lady Macbeth wills herself into evil, but the suppression of her conscience leaves nothing behind) should be introduced in Section 3 and used as the structural frame for Section 6. The Section 6 exam practice must explicitly connect 1.5 to 5.1 — students need to see the arc before building the essay.

2. **Section 3 — teach the gender context carefully:** "Unsex me here" is the most commonly misread quote in the play. Students must understand it means removing Jacobean-coded feminine qualities (compassion, hesitancy), not requesting a gender change. The TheoryCompareBlock comparing Jacobean feminine ideals with Lady Macbeth's behaviour is essential here.

3. **Section 4 — the milk/gall contrast is the episode's most teachable AO2 moment:** It appears in the invocation ("take my milk for gall") and reverberates in 5.1 (she cannot wash the blood from her hands). The MatchingTask should make this arc explicit: every quote in this episode has a 5.1 counterpart.

4. **Section 6 — this episode feeds the highest-profile past paper question (Jun 2024):** The exam practice here should be explicit about the AQA Jun 2024 question format. Use the indicative content from the mark scheme. Give students the essay structure before they try to write it.

5. **Very High retrieval priority — increase Section 5 depth:** 5 questions minimum on the QuickRecallScreen. Include at least one question that requires students to link 1.5 to 5.1 (the full arc) — this is the mark-scheme-winning whole-play move.

6. **Concealment continuity must be explicit:** Macbeth asks darkness to hide his desires in Episode 4; Lady Macbeth asks darkness and spirits to remake her body and conscience in Episode 5. This should be taught as a paired pattern, not two isolated quotes.
