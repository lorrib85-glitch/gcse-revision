### Section 2 — Plot & Dramatic Action
- **Purpose:** Teach the entrance as a sequence of stagecraft decisions: doorbell, lighting, physical impression, death announcement, photograph, questioning method, status clash.
- **Proposed content:**
  - Doorbell interruption: Inspector arrives after Birling dismisses collective responsibility; structure makes him the answer to Birling
  - Lighting: "pink and intimate" becomes "brighter and harder"; the room's moral temperature changes
  - Entrance description: "need not be a big man" but creates "massiveness, solidity and purposefulness"
  - Speech style: "speaks carefully, weightily" and looks hard at the person he addresses
  - Announcement: Eva has died after swallowing disinfectant; the Inspector makes suffering unavoidable
  - "Burnt her inside out, of course" — harsh language cuts through the family's comfort
  - Photograph: only selected characters see it; the audience never does; reactions become evidence
  - Method: "one person and one line of inquiry at a time"; the Inspector controls structure and prevents group defence
  - Birling attempts status defence: magistrate/Chief Constable connections; Inspector remains unmoved
  - Responsibility challenge: "Public men, Mr Birling, have responsibilities as well as privileges"
- **Suggested component(s):**
  - `VisualNarrativeScreen` — beat sequence: Birling speech → doorbell → lighting shift → Inspector enters → death announced → photograph → method explained → status challenge

## 4. Build recommendations

1. **Storyline integration:** The Inspector's identity question should be seeded but not closed. The learner should understand several readings and explain what each reading helps Priestley achieve.

2. **Section 2 — pace the reveal:** Do not rush to his final socialist message. Teach the entrance physically and theatrically first: doorbell, lighting, stage direction, gaze, death announcement, photograph, method.

3. **Section 4 — prioritise function:** "Massiveness, solidity and purposefulness" is the priority quote, but students must link it to function: moral force, not just powerful personality.

4. **Section 5 — avoid the weak paragraph:** "The Inspector is powerful because he is massive" is not enough. The better paragraph: Priestley uses the entrance stage directions to make the Inspector seem morally weighty, allowing him to overpower Birling's class/legal authority.

5. **Whole-play link requirement:** The Inspector's arrival and the final phone call frame the play. Every exam paragraph should link entrance → final speech or entrance → hoax debate/final phone call.

6. **Design:** Use a controlled lighting transition: warm pink-gold room falls into hard green-white truth as the Inspector enters. Do not make him cartoonishly supernatural; preserve ambiguity.
