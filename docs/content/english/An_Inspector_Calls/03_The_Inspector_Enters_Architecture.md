### Section 4 — Quote Analysis (AO2)
- **Proposed content:**

## 4. Build recommendations

1. **Storyline integration:** The Inspector's identity question should be seeded in Section 2 but not answered — the learner should feel the ambiguity. In Section 3, introduce him as an allegorical/morality play figure without closing the question down. In Section 5, the exam paragraph should acknowledge multiple interpretations ("the Inspector can be read as..."). The ambiguity is an exam strength, not a problem.

2. **Section 2 — pace the reveal:** Don't rush to his socialist message. Teach his physical entrance, his announcement, his method. The power builds through accumulation.

3. **Section 4 — "massiveness, solidity and purposefulness" is the priority quote:** It is the most exam-cited Inspector quote at this stage. Make sure learners can not only quote it but analyse all three nouns separately and contrast them with Birling's "heavy-looking."

4. **Section 5 — function over personality:** The highest-level AIC answers describe the Inspector's function (moral force, socialist mouthpiece, allegorical figure) not just his personality traits (forceful, determined). Build this into the exam paragraph scaffold.

5. **Whole-play link requirement:** The Inspector's arrival (here) and departure/phone call (Episode 11) are the structural frame of the play. Establishing this frame in Episode 3 gives learners a whole-play architecture they can use in any essay.
