## 4. Build recommendations

1. **Storyline integration:** Sheila's arc must be presented as a complete journey — from "very pleased with life" to "it frightens me." Section 2 shows the start; Section 3 names the change; Section 5's exam paragraph should show the end.

2. **The pseudo-Inspector role is exam gold:** Many students miss this. Build it explicitly into Section 2 — Sheila starts asking questions herself, warning others, refusing to pretend. Name it so learners can use the term.

3. **Gender must be handled carefully:** Her power to have Eva sacked comes from her class position, not legal power. This is an important nuance — she is herself constrained by gender in 1912, but she still has power over a working-class woman below her.

4. **Whole-play link:** The grade 9 model essay describes Sheila's final silence as "the silence of defeat, not guilt." This is a sophisticated reading — build it into Section 5 as the conclusion of the model paragraph.
