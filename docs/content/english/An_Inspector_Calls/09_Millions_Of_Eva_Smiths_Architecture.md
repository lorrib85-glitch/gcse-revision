## 4. Build recommendations

1. **Make it feel climactic:** This is the argumentative climax. Use space, fewer screens, and heavier retrieval.

2. **Quote accuracy matters:** Drill the exact lines. This passage is exam gold.

3. **Avoid history dump:** Teach 1945/46 context only through audience effect: they had lived through war and were debating social reform.

4. **Use the Birling contrast:** Birling's individualism vs Inspector's collective responsibility is the easiest high-mark whole-play link.

5. **Prepare the next chapters:** Episodes 10–11 test who has learned the speech and who tries to wriggle out.

6. **Design:** Use a grave, collective image language: crowd silhouettes, thread/chain/body motif, dim stage light. No political poster pastiche or neon sermon styling.
