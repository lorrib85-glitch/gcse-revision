## 4. Build recommendations

1. **This is the series' emotional and argumentative climax:** Every screen in this episode should feel like the culmination of Episodes 1–8. The chain of events has been built; now it is named and universalised. Section 2 should have weight and space.

2. **Quote accuracy is essential here:** The Inspector's speech is the most quoted passage in AIC. Every key line must be drilled for exact recall. Use `InteractiveCollectionExplorer` to surface the five key extracts and drill them.

3. **"Fire and blood and anguish" needs full AO3 treatment:** For a 1945 audience, this phrase is not a prediction — it is a lived memory. Teach this explicitly. The more precisely the AO3 context is integrated ("a 1945 audience who had just survived six years of war would hear this as..."), the higher the mark.

4. **Contrast with Episode 2:** The natural whole-play link is Birling's "community and all that nonsense" vs the Inspector's "we are members of one body." This contrast should feature in Section 5's model paragraph.
