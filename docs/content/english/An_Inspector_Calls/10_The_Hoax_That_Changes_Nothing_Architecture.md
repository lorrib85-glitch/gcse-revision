## 4. Build recommendations

1. **The morality vs legality distinction is the key teaching point:** Make it explicit. Name it. Use the exact text to prove it (Sheila's lines are the clearest). Then use the exam paragraph to practise arguing it.

2. **Don't let Gerald off easily:** His reversion to the older generation's logic is the episode's most revealing character moment. His "everything's all right now, Sheila" is the line that shows what he is.

3. **Birling's knighthood returns:** Bring back the knighthood reference from Episode 2. His first thought after the "hoax" is his knighthood — this is the most efficient character revelation Priestley can make in two words.

4. **Structural purpose:** This episode's structural function (a false resolution before the real ending) must be taught. Without understanding why Priestley inserts this false comfort, the phone call in Episode 11 loses its power.
