## 4. Build recommendations

1. **Storyline integration:** The "chain of events" metaphor should be introduced in Section 2 and returned to in Section 6. By Episode 9 the learner will have built up the full chain; this is where it starts.

2. **"Twelve percent" is the key moment:** It is the moment when Priestley most nakedly exposes how capitalism reduces people to statistics. Make it the centrepiece of Section 4 — the analysis that a percentage is placed against a person's life is accessible and powerful for learners.

3. **Sheila's line as moral touchstone:** "these girls aren't cheap labour — they're people" is one of Sheila's most important moments. It pre-figures her full awakening in Episode 5. Flag it here and return to it in Episode 5.

4. **AO3 must be lean and purposeful:** The context needed here is: no minimum wage in 1912, trade unions suppressed, employers could sack workers at will. That is enough. Don't turn this into an industrial history lesson.
