### Section 2 — Learning Chunk 1
- **Purpose:** Introduce Malcolm X — his background, NOI philosophy, and contrast with MLK.
- **Proposed content for this episode:**
  - Malcolm X's background: father killed, mother institutionalised, prison, NOI conversion
  - NOI philosophy: Black separatism, Black pride, rejection of integration, "by any means necessary"
  - The contrast with MLK: different geography (North vs South), different audience (urban poor vs church-going), different philosophy (self-defence vs non-violence, separatism vs integration)
  - His appeal to Northern urban Black Americans: the civil rights movement had focused on Southern Jim Crow; Northern poverty, de facto segregation and police brutality were different problems that required different answers
- **Suggested component(s):**
  - `VisualNarrativeScreen` — beat-by-beat narrative of Malcolm X: childhood (father killed, mother institutionalised) → prison → NOI conversion → national spokesman → "by any means necessary" → Mecca pilgrimage → revised views → assassination; cinematic, single throughline
  - `QuickRecallScreen` — retrieval of Northern segregation (Episode 1: de facto vs de jure) and limits of 1964 Act (Episode 5) interleaved with Malcolm X facts; connects why his message resonated in the North

### Section 3 — Learning Chunk 2
- **Purpose:** Black Power — origins, Carmichael, and the movement's internal fracture.
- **Proposed content for this episode:**
  - James Meredith shot (June 1966) and the March Against Fear: SNCC and SCLC leaders continue the march; Carmichael coins "Black Power" to the crowd
  - What Black Power meant: Black pride, Black political/economic self-sufficiency, separatism, rejection of white liberal allies
  - SNCC under Carmichael: expelled white members; lost funding; shifted from direct action to political organising
  - John Lewis's toned-down March on Washington speech (Episode 5) as a bridge: the same SNCC radicalism had been building since 1963
  - Interleaving: connect Stokely Carmichael's Freedom Ride participation (Episode 4) to his Black Power coinage — the same person, the same frustration with the limits of non-violence
- **Suggested component(s):**
  - `ExplainReveal` — cause-and-effect chain: limits of 1964 Act (no voting rights, no economic equality) + Northern poverty and riots + MLK's non-violence seen as inadequate → Black Power as the alternative philosophy; reveals each step progressively
  - `QuickRecallScreen` — retrieval of Freedom Rides (Carmichael was a rider, Episode 4) interleaved with Black Power facts; the same person at different stages of radicalisation

### Section 4 — Learning Chunk 3
- **Purpose:** The Black Panther Party — both dimensions: community programmes and militant tactics.
- **Proposed content for this episode:**
  - Founded by Newton and Seale (Oakland, October 1966): the context of Bay Area police brutality
  - Armed patrols and "policing the police": California open-carry law used deliberately; legal but provocative
  - Ten-point plan: full employment, decent housing, Black exemption from military, end to police brutality
  - Community programmes: free breakfast clubs (10,000 children daily at peak), health clinics, education
  - COINTELPRO: FBI classified Panthers as "greatest internal security threat"; targeted breakfast clubs as priority; used infiltration and disinformation
- **Suggested component(s):**
  - `InteractiveCollectionExplorer` — explore "The Black Panther Party" (armed patrols / ten-point plan / free breakfast clubs / health clinics / COINTELPRO response); colour-coded by type; makes the two dimensions (community + militant) visible simultaneously — directly prepares for 2022 Q3
  - `MatchingTask` — match Black Panther element to dimension: (free breakfast clubs → community programme), (armed patrols → self-defence/militant), (ten-point plan → political manifesto), (COINTELPRO → federal response to Panthers' influence); active processing for Q3 evaluation

### Section 5 — Learning Chunk 4

  - `QuickRecallScreen` — retrieval of full episode content: Malcolm X, Mecca, Black Power coinage, SNCC, Black Panthers (both dimensions), Watts, Kerner Report, 1968 Olympics

## 4. Build recommendations

1. **The 2022 Q3 Black Panthers interpretation as the exam anchor:** This episode contains the content for the most recently examined Q3(d) question in the series (2022). Section 4's `InteractiveCollectionExplorer` and Section 6's `ExaminerExplainsScreen` should explicitly reference the evaluation structure needed for this question type. The two dimensions of the Panthers (community programmes / militant tactics) are the two halves of the exam answer.

2. **Malcolm X's Mecca evolution — not just "by any means necessary":** The VisualNarrativeScreen in Section 2 should include the Mecca pilgrimage and revised views. Students who only know "by any means necessary" without knowing that Malcolm X was evolving toward a broader human rights framework miss marks on "how far do you agree" questions. The assassination cut off an evolution that was heading toward a more collaborative position.

3. **Carmichael as a bridge across episodes:** The `ExplainReveal` in Section 3 and the `QuickRecallScreen` interleaving should explicitly link Carmichael's Freedom Rider past (Episode 4) to his Black Power coinage. This is the clearest example in the series of a person changing their approach in response to the movement's failures — and it earns marks in Q2 "why did Black Power emerge" questions.

4. **COINTELPRO as a hidden thread:** The FBI's targeting of the Panthers' breakfast programmes is counterintuitive (why would the government worry about free food for children?) but reveals the government's real fear — that the Panthers were building popular loyalty. Including this in the `InteractiveCollectionExplorer` and the `ColSortBlock` gives students a distinctive, high-mark detail for Q3(d) answers.

5. **This episode introduces Q3(d) structure for the first time:** Sections 5 and 6 should explicitly model the "how far do you agree" answer structure, because this is the first episode where a Q3(d) question maps directly onto the episode's content (Black Panthers 2022). The `ExaminerExplainsScreen` and `FaceTheExaminer` should make the evaluation structure explicit.
