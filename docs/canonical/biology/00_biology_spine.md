# Biology canonical spine

Course: AQA GCSE Combined Science Trilogy Biology
Purpose: 40-week Biology sequence. 4 modules. 10 chapters per module. 40 chapters total.
Audience: GCSE student, approx. age 15.
Tone: cinematic, clear, science-serious, teen-accessible. Interesting but not childish.

---

## Module 1 — The living machine

Big idea: How life is built, how cells work, and how substances move.
AQA coverage: Cell biology, cell transport.

| # | Title | Coverage |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | The secret world inside you | Animal, plant and bacterial cells; organelles and their functions; eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells. |
| 2 | Zooming into the invisible | Light microscopes, electron microscopes, magnification, resolution, scale and size. |
| 3 | The microscope mission | Required practical — preparing slides, observing cells, drawing cells, labelling cells, calculating magnification. |
| 4 | Built for one job | Specialised cells in animals and plants: sperm, nerve, muscle, root hair, xylem and phloem. |
| 5 | The body's copy machine | Chromosomes, DNA, the cell cycle, mitosis, growth and repair. |
| 6 | Medicine's repair kit | Stem cells, embryonic stem cells, adult stem cells, meristems, therapeutic cloning, risks and ethics. |
| 7 | The great particle escape | Diffusion, concentration gradients, exchange surfaces, surface area to volume ratio. |
| 8 | The potato water test | Osmosis and required practical — plant tissue mass change, percentage change, variables, graphs and conclusions. |
| 9 | Against the flow | Active transport, mineral ion uptake in roots, glucose absorption in the gut, energy from respiration. |
| 10 | Cell biology boss level | Exam practice chapter for cells, microscopy and transport; magnification calculations; required practical recall; diffusion/osmosis/active transport comparisons. |

## Module 2 — Body systems under pressure

Big idea: How the human body digests, transports, breathes, gets damaged and fights disease.
AQA coverage: Organisation, infection and response.

| # | Title | Coverage |
|---|-------|----------|
| 11 | From tiny cells to full-body systems | Cells, tissues, organs, organ systems; principles of organisation. |
| 12 | The food breakdown machine | Digestive system, digestion, absorption, enzymes, lock-and-key model, carbohydrases, proteases, lipases, bile. |
| 13 | CSI: Food samples | Required practical — food tests for sugars, starch, proteins and lipids; Benedict's, iodine, Biuret and appropriate observations. |
| 14 | The enzyme danger zone | Required practical — effect of pH on amylase; starch breakdown; iodine spotting tile; temperature control; variables; denaturation. |
| 15 | The double pump | Heart structure, double circulatory system, arteries, veins, capillaries, blood flow, pacemakers. |
| 16 | The oxygen swap | Lungs, trachea, bronchi, alveoli, capillaries, ventilation, gas exchange and diffusion. |
| 17 | Blocked supply lines | Coronary heart disease, fatty deposits, stents, statins, faulty valves, transplants and artificial hearts. |
| 18 | Risk, damage and runaway cells | Health, lifestyle risk factors, correlation and causation, cancer, benign and malignant tumours. |
| 19 | The invaders | Communicable disease, pathogens, viral diseases, bacterial diseases, fungal diseases and protist diseases. |
| 20 | The body fights back | Human defences, immune system, white blood cells, antibodies, antitoxins, vaccination, antibiotics, painkillers and antibiotic resistance. |

## Module 3 — Power and control

Big idea: How organisms release energy and keep internal conditions stable.
AQA coverage: Bioenergetics, homeostasis and response.

| # | Title | Coverage |
|---|-------|----------|
| 21 | Sunlight into sugar | Photosynthesis, chloroplasts, chlorophyll, word equation and symbol equation. |
| 22 | The bottleneck problem | Limiting factors of photosynthesis: light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration and temperature; required practical — effect of light intensity on photosynthesis; graphs and evaluation. |
| 23 | What plants do with the sugar | Uses of glucose from photosynthesis: respiration, starch, cellulose, amino acids/proteins, lipids/oils. |
| 24 | Releasing the energy | Aerobic respiration, anaerobic respiration in muscles, anaerobic respiration in yeast, fermentation. |
| 25 | When your muscles run out of oxygen | Exercise response, increased heart rate and breathing rate, lactic acid, oxygen debt, metabolism. |
| 26 | The body's thermostat | Homeostasis, internal conditions, control systems, receptors, coordination centres, effectors and responses. |
| 27 | Instant messages in your nerves | Nervous system, CNS, neurones, reflex arc, synapses, fast responses. |
| 28 | How fast are your reactions? | Required practical — human reaction time; ruler drop method; repeats; variables; evaluating method; mean and anomalies. |
| 29 | Chemical control messages | Endocrine system, hormones, glands, blood glucose control, pancreas, insulin and glucagon. |
| 30 | Control of reproduction | Reproductive hormones, menstrual cycle, contraception, fertility treatment, IVF evaluation. |

## Module 4 — Life, evolution and planet Earth

Big idea: How life is inherited, changes over time, and survives in ecosystems.
AQA coverage: Inheritance, variation and evolution; ecology.

| # | Title | Coverage |
|---|-------|----------|
| 31 | The code hidden in every cell | DNA, genes, chromosomes, genome, alleles, genetic information. |
| 32 | Making a new human | Sexual and asexual reproduction, meiosis, gametes, fertilisation and variation. |
| 33 | Inheritance without panic | Dominant and recessive alleles, genotype, phenotype, Punnett squares and probability. |
| 34 | Genetic chance and genetic conditions | Inherited disorders, polydactyly, cystic fibrosis, sex determination, XX and XY chromosomes, embryo screening evaluation. |
| 35 | Survival of the best-suited | Variation, environmental and genetic causes, natural selection and evolution. |
| 36 | Designing life | Selective breeding, genetic engineering, benefits, risks and examples in plants, animals and microorganisms. |
| 37 | Evolution caught in the act | Evidence for evolution, fossils, extinction, resistant bacteria and classification. |
| 38 | The survival network | Ecosystems, communities, populations, interdependence, competition, abiotic factors, biotic factors and adaptations. |
| 39 | Fieldwork: counting life | Required practical — ecology sampling, quadrats, transects, estimating population size/distribution, validity and evaluation. |
| 40 | Planet under pressure | Carbon cycle, water cycle, decomposition, biodiversity, waste management, land use, deforestation, global warming and maintaining biodiversity. |
