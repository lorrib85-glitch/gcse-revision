## Big question

How does a single cell become a whole working body — and why does organisation at every level matter?

## Key concepts / vocabulary

- **Cell:** the basic structural and functional unit of life
- **Tissue:** a group of similar cells with a similar structure and function, working together to carry out a specific job
- **Organ:** a structure made of different tissues working together to perform a specific function, such as the stomach, heart or leaf
- **Organ system:** a group of organs working together to perform a larger body function, such as digestion or circulation
- **Organism:** a complete living thing made up of one or more cells
- **Muscular tissue:** tissue that contracts to produce movement; in the stomach it churns food
- **Glandular tissue:** tissue that secretes substances such as enzymes, hormones or acid; in the stomach it produces digestive juices
- **Epithelial tissue:** tissue that covers the surface of an organ; it lines the stomach and protects/covers surfaces
- **Digestive system:** an organ system that breaks food into small soluble molecules and absorbs them into the blood
- **Mesophyll tissue:** leaf tissue where photosynthesis takes place; includes palisade mesophyll and spongy mesophyll
- **Palisade mesophyll:** leaf tissue packed with chloroplasts, adapted for photosynthesis
- **Spongy mesophyll:** leaf tissue with air spaces, adapted for gas diffusion
- **Xylem:** plant tissue that transports water and mineral ions from roots to leaves
- **Phloem:** plant tissue that transports dissolved sugars from leaves to the rest of the plant
- **Epidermal tissue:** the outer covering of a plant organ; protects the plant and reduces water loss
- **Guard cells:** cells that open and close stomata, controlling gas exchange and water loss
- **Stomata:** pores in the leaf that allow gases to move in and out

## Core explanation

A multicellular organism is not just a pile of cells.

Cells specialise. Similar specialised cells group together into tissues. Different tissues work together as organs. Organs cooperate in organ systems. All the organ systems together make the organism.

The hierarchy is:

> cell → tissue → organ → organ system → organism

AQA likes students to explain the difference between each level precisely:

- a tissue is made of similar cells
- an organ is made of different tissues
- an organ system is made of different organs

### Example: stomach as an organ

The stomach is an organ because it contains different tissues working together.

| Tissue | Function in the stomach |
|---|---|
| Muscular tissue | contracts to churn/mix food |
| Glandular tissue | secretes digestive enzymes and hydrochloric acid |
| Epithelial tissue | covers and protects the stomach surface |

The stomach is part of the digestive system.

### Example: digestive system as an organ system

The digestive system is an organ system because several organs work together to digest food and absorb soluble products.

Organs include:

- mouth
- oesophagus
- stomach
- liver
- gall bladder
- pancreas
- small intestine
- large intestine

This system breaks large insoluble food molecules into small soluble molecules that can be absorbed into the blood.

### Example: leaf as a plant organ

A leaf is a plant organ because it is made of different tissues working together for photosynthesis and gas exchange.

| Tissue | Function in the leaf |
|---|---|
| Epidermal tissue | covers and protects the leaf |
| Palisade mesophyll | contains many chloroplasts for photosynthesis |
| Spongy mesophyll | has air spaces for gas diffusion |
| Xylem | brings water and mineral ions to the leaf |
| Phloem | carries dissolved sugars away from the leaf |
| Guard cells/stomata | control gas exchange and water loss |

Plants also have organs and organ systems:

- roots absorb water and mineral ions
- stems support the plant and transport substances
- leaves carry out photosynthesis
- root and shoot systems work together to keep the plant alive

## Likely misconceptions

- MISCONCEPTION: "All cells in an organ are identical." Correction: an organ contains different types of tissue, each made of cells suited to different jobs. For example, the stomach contains muscular, glandular and epithelial tissue.
- MISCONCEPTION: "A tissue is just any random group of cells." Correction: a tissue is a group of similar cells with a similar structure and function.
- MISCONCEPTION: "An organ is a group of cells." Correction: an organ is made of different tissues, not just one cell type.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Organs work independently." Correction: organs work within organ systems, and organ systems are interdependent. For example, the digestive system depends on the circulatory system to distribute absorbed nutrients.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Only animals have organs." Correction: plants also have organs such as roots, stems and leaves.
- MISCONCEPTION: "The heart is a muscle." Correction: the heart is an organ made of several tissues, including cardiac muscle tissue. It is not just one tissue type.
- MISCONCEPTION: "The digestive system is one organ." Correction: the digestive system is an organ system made of several organs working together.

2 marks

- with a similar structure and function / working together to perform a function

### Question 2

Explain why the stomach is described as an organ.  
3 marks

Mark points:

- made of different tissues
- tissues work together
- to perform digestion / churn food / produce digestive juices

### Question 3

Name three tissues found in the stomach and give the function of each.  
3 marks

Mark points:

- muscular tissue contracts/churns food
- glandular tissue secretes enzymes/acid
- epithelial tissue covers/protects stomach surface

### Question 4

A leaf is a plant organ. Explain why.  
3 marks

Mark points:

- made of different tissues
- tissues work together
- examples: mesophyll for photosynthesis, xylem for water transport, phloem for sugar transport, epidermis for protection

### Question 5

Describe the levels of organisation in a human, from cell to organism.  
4 marks

Mark points:

- cells form tissues
- tissues form organs
- organs form organ systems
- organ systems form an organism
