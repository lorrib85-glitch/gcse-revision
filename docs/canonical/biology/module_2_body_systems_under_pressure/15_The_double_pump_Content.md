# Chapter 15: The double pump — Content

## Series: Biology Module 2 — Body systems under pressure (AQA GCSE Combined Science Trilogy 8464, Biology Paper 1)

---

## Big question

Why does the heart need to pump blood in two separate loops — and what happens when it can't?

---

## Syllabus coverage

- The heart as a double pump: right side pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs; left side pumps oxygenated blood to the body
- Heart structure: four chambers (right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, left ventricle); septum; valves (atrioventricular and semilunar)
- Major blood vessels: aorta, vena cava, pulmonary artery, pulmonary vein
- The two circulatory loops: pulmonary circulation (heart → lungs → heart) and systemic circulation (heart → body → heart)
- The role of the pacemaker: sinoatrial node (SAN) controls the resting heart rate; artificial pacemakers for irregular heartbeat
- Structure and function of blood vessels: arteries (thick walls, elastic, carry blood away from heart under high pressure), veins (thinner walls, valves to prevent backflow, carry blood to heart under low pressure), capillaries (one cell thick, large surface area, exchange of substances)

---

## Key concepts / vocabulary

- **Atrium (pl. atria):** upper chamber of the heart that receives blood
- **Ventricle:** lower chamber of the heart that pumps blood out
- **Septum:** the wall dividing the left and right sides of the heart; prevents mixing of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood
- **Valve:** a flap that prevents backflow of blood; found between chambers and at the exits from the ventricles
- **Aorta:** the large artery leaving the left ventricle that carries oxygenated blood to the body
- **Vena cava:** the large vein returning deoxygenated blood from the body to the right atrium
- **Pulmonary artery:** carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle to the lungs
- **Pulmonary vein:** carries oxygenated blood from the lungs back to the left atrium
- **Double circulatory system:** a circulatory system with two separate loops — pulmonary (heart to lungs and back) and systemic (heart to body and back)
- **Sinoatrial node (SAN):** the heart's natural pacemaker; generates electrical impulses that coordinate the heartbeat
- **Artery:** a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart; thick, elastic walls; carries blood under high pressure
- **Vein:** a blood vessel that carries blood towards the heart; thinner walls; contains valves to prevent backflow; lower pressure
- **Capillary:** the smallest blood vessel; walls one cell thick; allows exchange of substances (oxygen, glucose, carbon dioxide, waste) between blood and tissues
- **Coronary arteries:** arteries that supply the heart muscle itself with oxygen and glucose

## Likely misconceptions

- MISCONCEPTION: "The left side of the heart pumps blood to the lungs." Correction: the right side of the heart pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs. The left side pumps oxygenated blood to the body. Students frequently swap these.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Arteries always carry oxygenated blood." Correction: arteries carry blood away from the heart. The pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood from the heart to the lungs. The defining feature of arteries is direction, not oxygen content.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Veins always carry deoxygenated blood." Correction: the pulmonary vein carries oxygenated blood from the lungs to the heart. Again, direction — not oxygen content — defines the vessel type.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Valves are only found between the heart chambers." Correction: valves are also found at the exits from the ventricles (semilunar valves in the aorta and pulmonary artery) and in veins throughout the body.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Capillaries have the thickest walls because they are most important." Correction: capillaries have walls one cell thick — the thinnest — which allows efficient diffusion of substances between blood and cells. Arteries have the thickest walls.
- MISCONCEPTION: "The heart is an artery/vein." Correction: the heart is an organ (a pump). Arteries and veins are blood vessels that connect to the heart.
