## 4. Content reference pack

### Exam question bank for this chapter

**Magnification calculations (Ch 2):**
1. A cell image is 45 mm long. The actual cell is 0.05 mm. Calculate the magnification. (M = 45/0.05 = ×900)
2. A scale bar represents 10 µm and measures 20 mm on the image. A structure measures 3 mm. Calculate its actual size. (Magnification = 20/0.01 = ×2000; actual size = 3/2000 = 0.0015 mm = 1.5 µm)
3. Magnification ×500. Cell image 30 mm. Actual size? (30/500 = 0.06 mm = 60 µm)

**Define/describe questions:**
- "Define diffusion." (2 marks — movement of particles from higher to lower concentration by random particle movement; passive)
- "Define osmosis." (2 marks — movement of water molecules from higher to lower water potential through a partially permeable membrane; passive)
- "Define active transport." (2 marks — movement of particles from lower to higher concentration against concentration gradient; using energy from respiration; carrier proteins)
- "What is a stem cell?" (2 marks — undifferentiated cell; can self-renew by mitosis; can differentiate into specialised cells)
- "What is the difference between eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells?" (2 marks — eukaryotic has membrane-bound nucleus; prokaryotic does not)

**Adaptation questions (Ch 4):**
- "Give two ways a sperm cell is adapted for fertilisation." (2 marks — any 2 of: tail for swimming; many mitochondria for energy; acrosome with enzymes)
- "Explain why root hair cells do not contain chloroplasts." (2 marks — underground; no light; photosynthesis impossible; chloroplasts not needed)
- "Describe one structural adaptation of a xylem cell and explain how it aids water transport." (2 marks — any one adaptation with explanation)

**Required practical questions (Ch 3 and Ch 8):**
- "Describe how you would prepare a slide to observe cheek cells. Include why you add methylene blue." (3 marks)
- "A student's potato cylinder changes from 5.0 g to 4.3 g. Calculate the percentage change in mass." (2 marks — (4.3−5.0)/5.0 × 100 = −14%)
- "The student found one result that did not fit the trend on their graph. What should the student do with this anomalous result?" (1–2 marks — do not include in line of best fit; investigate/repeat that concentration)
- "Suggest one reason why a student's results might be unreliable in the osmosis investigation." (1–2 marks — did not dry cylinders consistently; different initial masses used; temperature varied)

**Three-way comparison (Ch 7, 8, 9):**
- "Compare diffusion and active transport. In your answer, include: direction of movement, energy requirements, and one example of each." (4 marks)
- "Explain one similarity and one difference between diffusion and osmosis." (2 marks)
- "Explain why active transport, but not diffusion, requires energy." (2 marks — diffusion is passive; moves with gradient; active transport moves against gradient; requires ATP from respiration)

**Cell cycle and stem cells (Ch 5, 6):**
- "State two reasons why mitosis is important." (2 marks — growth; repair; replacement; asexual reproduction — any two)
- "Explain what happens during the cell cycle before mitosis begins." (3 marks — cell grows; DNA is replicated; cell grows further in preparation for division)
- "Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of using embryonic stem cells in medicine." (4–6 marks — balanced answer)

### Key exam technique notes

**Command words (AQA):**
- "State" — give a fact; no explanation needed; usually 1 mark
- "Define" — give a precise meaning; usually 2 marks (two components needed)
- "Describe" — give a factual account; no explanation required; multi-mark usually needs multiple points
- "Explain" — give a reason; "because" / "so that" / "therefore" should appear
- "Compare" — state similarities AND differences; must mention both
- "Discuss" — present both sides; balanced argument; conclusion expected in 6-mark questions
- "Calculate" — must show working; give units; correct significant figures

**Common mark-loss patterns for Module 1:**
- Saying "mitochondria MAKE energy" → deducted; correct phrase is "release energy" or "site of aerobic respiration"
- Omitting "passive" from diffusion/osmosis definitions
- Omitting "water molecules" from osmosis definition (just saying "water" often still credited, but "particles" would lose a mark)
- Direction error in active transport definition (high → low instead of low → high)
- Percentage change: dividing by final mass instead of initial mass
- Magnification: mixing units (mm and µm) in the same calculation
- "Diffusion requires energy" → FALSE; 0 marks
- "All plant cells have chloroplasts" → always false in a GCSE context; mark for specific cell type
