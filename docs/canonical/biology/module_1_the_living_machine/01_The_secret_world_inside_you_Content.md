# Chapter 1: The secret world inside you — Content

## Series: Biology Module 1 — The living machine (AQA GCSE Combined Science Trilogy 8464, Biology Paper 1)

---

## Big question

What do a leaf cell, a blood cell and a bacterium all have in common — and what makes each one fundamentally different?

---

## Syllabus coverage

- Animal cell structures: nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria, ribosomes
- Plant cell structures: all animal cell structures plus cell wall (cellulose), chloroplasts, permanent vacuole
- Bacterial (prokaryotic) cell structures: cytoplasm, cell membrane, cell wall, ribosomes, single circular DNA loop, plasmids
- Eukaryotic cells: animal and plant cells — genetic material enclosed in a membrane-bound nucleus
- Prokaryotic cells: bacterial cells — no nucleus; DNA is a single circular loop in the cytoplasm; may contain plasmids
- Function of each organelle using AQA-precise wording
- Comparison of all three cell types
- Size difference: bacterial cells much smaller (~1–10 µm); eukaryotic cells larger (~10–100 µm)

---

## Key concepts / vocabulary

- **Cell:** the basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms
- **Organelle:** a specialised structure inside a cell that performs a specific function
- **Nucleus:** contains the cell's DNA; controls the activities of the cell; present in eukaryotic cells only
- **Cytoplasm:** jelly-like substance in which most of the cell's chemical reactions take place; present in all cells
- **Cell membrane:** controls what substances move into and out of the cell; selectively permeable; present in all cells
- **Mitochondria:** the site of aerobic respiration, where energy is transferred for the cell to use; present in animal and plant cells
- **Ribosomes:** the site of protein synthesis; present in all cells including prokaryotes
- **Cell wall:** provides structural support and a rigid, fixed shape; made of cellulose in plant cells; also present in bacterial cells; not found in animal cells
- **Chloroplasts:** contain chlorophyll; absorb light energy for photosynthesis; found only in plant cells exposed to light
- **Permanent vacuole:** large water-filled sac containing cell sap; keeps the plant cell firm (turgid)
- **Cell sap:** the solution stored inside the permanent vacuole
- **Eukaryotic cell:** a cell whose genetic material is enclosed in a membrane-bound nucleus; includes animal and plant cells
- **Prokaryotic cell:** a cell with no membrane-bound nucleus; genetic material is a single circular DNA loop in the cytoplasm; includes bacterial cells
- **Plasmids:** small rings of DNA found in some bacterial cells, separate from the main circular chromosome
- **Selectively permeable:** allowing some substances through but not others; the key property of the cell membrane
- **Turgid:** firm; used to describe a plant cell with a full vacuole pushing against the cell wall

## Likely misconceptions

- MISCONCEPTION: "All plant cells contain chloroplasts." Correction: only cells exposed to light contain chloroplasts. Root hair cells, potato tuber cells and cells inside a stem receive no light and do not have chloroplasts.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Bacterial cells have a nucleus." Correction: bacterial cells are prokaryotic — their genetic material is a single circular DNA loop free in the cytoplasm, not enclosed in a nucleus.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Mitochondria make energy." Correction: mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration, where energy stored in glucose is transferred for the cell to use. Energy is released from chemical bonds, not created.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Ribosomes read DNA directly." Correction: AQA-safe phrasing is that ribosomes are the site of protein synthesis. At GCSE, avoid language implying direct DNA reading.
- MISCONCEPTION: "All cells are roughly the same size." Correction: cells vary enormously. Bacterial cells are typically 1–10 µm; eukaryotic cells 10–100 µm. A single motor neurone can extend over a metre.
- MISCONCEPTION: "The cell wall is only found in plant cells." Correction: bacterial cells also have a cell wall (though not made of cellulose). At GCSE the key contrast is that animal cells lack a cell wall entirely.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Prokaryotes are primitive versions of plant or animal cells." Correction: bacteria are a fundamentally different category — not a smaller or simpler version of either.
