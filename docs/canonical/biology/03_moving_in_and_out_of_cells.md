# Biology Chapter 3 — Moving in and out of cells

By the end, students should be able to:

## Core content

### Diffusion

Diffusion is the net movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration.

Key points:

- particles move randomly
- overall movement is down the concentration gradient
- no energy from respiration is required

Examples:

- oxygen diffuses from the lungs into the blood
- carbon dioxide diffuses from the blood into the lungs
- urea diffuses from cells into blood plasma before excretion by the kidneys

Diffusion is faster when:

- the concentration gradient is steeper
- temperature is higher
- surface area of the membrane is larger
- diffusion distance is shorter

### Surface area to volume ratio

Small organisms have a large surface area compared with their volume, so diffusion across the cell surface can be enough.

Large multicellular organisms have a smaller surface area to volume ratio, so they need specialised exchange surfaces and transport systems.

Effective exchange surfaces usually have:

- large surface area
- thin membrane for short diffusion distance
- good blood supply in animals
- ventilation for gas exchange in animals

Examples to cover:

- alveoli in the lungs
- villi in the small intestine
- gills in fish
- roots and leaves in plants

### Osmosis

AQA wording:

> Osmosis is the diffusion of water from a dilute solution to a concentrated solution through a partially permeable membrane.

Make clear:

- dilute solution = more water molecules
- concentrated solution = fewer free water molecules because more solute is dissolved
- partially permeable membrane lets water through but not all dissolved substances

Plant cells:

- if plant cells gain water, the vacuole fills and the cell becomes turgid
- if plant cells lose water, the vacuole shrinks and the cell becomes flaccid

### Required practical: osmosis in plant tissue

Required practical activity 2: investigate the effect of a range of concentrations of salt or sugar solutions on the mass of plant tissue.

Method summary:

1. Cut potato cylinders to the same size.
2. Measure and record the starting mass of each cylinder.
3. Place each cylinder into a different concentration of sugar or salt solution.
4. Leave for the same amount of time.
5. Remove cylinders and gently blot dry.
6. Measure final mass.
7. Calculate change in mass and percentage change.
8. Plot concentration against percentage change in mass.

Variables:

- independent variable: concentration of salt/sugar solution
- dependent variable: change in mass / percentage change in mass
- control variables: size of potato pieces, time in solution, temperature, volume of solution, potato type

Common exam traps:

- blot dry before weighing final mass
- use percentage change when starting masses differ
- repeat and calculate a mean for reliability

### Percentage change

Formula:

> percentage change = change in mass / original mass × 100

Worked example:

A potato cylinder has a starting mass of 2.50 g and a final mass of 2.80 g.

Change in mass = 2.80 - 2.50 = 0.30 g

Percentage change = 0.30 / 2.50 × 100 = 12%

Answer: 12% increase.

### Active transport

Active transport moves substances from a more dilute solution to a more concentrated solution, against the concentration gradient, using energy from respiration.

Students need to know:

- it moves against the concentration gradient
- it requires energy
- energy comes from respiration

Examples:

- mineral ions enter root hair cells from the soil by active transport
- glucose can be absorbed from the gut into the blood by active transport

### Comparing diffusion, osmosis and active transport

| Process | What moves? | Direction | Energy needed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Diffusion | particles | high to low concentration | no |
| Osmosis | water only | dilute to concentrated solution through partially permeable membrane | no |
| Active transport | dissolved substances e.g. mineral ions/glucose | low to high concentration | yes |

### Percentage change builder

Question:

A potato cylinder starts at 4.00 g and ends at 3.60 g. Calculate percentage change.

Steps:

1. Change = 3.60 - 4.00 = -0.40 g
2. Percentage change = -0.40 / 4.00 × 100
3. Answer = -10%
4. The potato lost mass.

## Exam practice

### Question 1 — diffusion

Explain how oxygen moves from the alveoli into the blood.  
4 marks

Mark points:

- oxygen concentration is higher in alveoli than blood
- oxygen diffuses down the concentration gradient
- alveoli have thin walls / short diffusion distance
- alveoli have large surface area
- blood supply maintains concentration gradient

### Question 2 — osmosis practical

A student investigates the effect of sugar solution concentration on potato cylinders.

Describe how the student should carry out the investigation.  
6 marks

Mark points:

- cut equal-sized potato cylinders
- measure starting mass
- place in different concentrations of sugar solution
- keep time/temperature/volume/size controlled
- remove and blot dry
- measure final mass
- calculate percentage change in mass
- repeat and calculate mean

### Question 3 — active transport

Root hair cells absorb mineral ions from the soil by active transport.

Explain why active transport is needed.  
3 marks

Mark points:

- mineral ion concentration may be lower in soil than inside root hair cells
- ions move against the concentration gradient
- energy from respiration is required

## Final retrieval

1. What is diffusion?  
Net movement of particles from high concentration to low concentration.

2. Name two factors that increase diffusion rate.  
Any two: steeper concentration gradient, higher temperature, larger surface area, shorter diffusion distance.

3. What is osmosis?  
Diffusion of water from a dilute solution to a concentrated solution through a partially permeable membrane.

4. Why blot potato cylinders dry before weighing?  
To remove surface solution so it does not affect the mass.

5. What is active transport?  
Movement against a concentration gradient using energy from respiration.

6. Which process moves water only?  
Osmosis.
