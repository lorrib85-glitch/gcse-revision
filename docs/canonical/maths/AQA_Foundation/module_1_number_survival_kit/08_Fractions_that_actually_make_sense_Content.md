## Key concepts and vocabulary

- **Numerator:** the top number of a fraction; how many parts are being considered
- **Denominator:** the bottom number of a fraction; how many equal parts the whole is divided into
- **Equivalent fractions:** fractions that represent the same value but have different numerators and denominators; e.g. 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 50/100
- **Simplest form / lowest terms:** a fraction where the HCF of numerator and denominator is 1; e.g. 6/8 simplifies to 3/4 (dividing by HCF = 2)
- **Improper fraction:** a fraction where the numerator is greater than the denominator (value > 1); e.g. 7/4
- **Mixed number:** a whole number combined with a proper fraction; e.g. 1¾ = 7/4
- **Common denominator:** a shared multiple of the denominators of two fractions; used when ordering or adding fractions
- **Fraction of an amount:** e.g. 3/5 of 40 = (40 ÷ 5) × 3 = 8 × 3 = 24
- **Unit fraction:** a fraction with numerator 1; e.g. 1/4, 1/7
- **Proper fraction:** a fraction where the numerator is less than the denominator (value < 1)

## Required methods

### Equivalent fractions
Multiply or divide numerator and denominator by the **same** non-zero integer:
- 1/4 = 3/12 (multiply both by 3)
- 20/30 = 2/3 (divide both by 10)

### Simplifying to lowest terms
1. Find the HCF of numerator and denominator (from Ch6)
2. Divide both by the HCF

**Example:** 18/24 → HCF(18,24) = 6 → 18 ÷ 6 = 3; 24 ÷ 6 = 4 → simplified: 3/4

### Converting between improper fractions and mixed numbers
- **Improper → mixed:** divide numerator by denominator; quotient = whole number part; remainder = new numerator
  - 17/5 → 17 ÷ 5 = 3 remainder 2 → 3 and 2/5
- **Mixed → improper:** (whole × denominator) + numerator = new numerator; same denominator
  - 2 and 3/4 → (2 × 4) + 3 = 11 → 11/4

### Ordering fractions
1. Convert all fractions to a common denominator (use LCM of denominators)
2. Compare numerators

**Example:** Order 3/4, 2/3, 5/6 → common denominator 12: 9/12, 8/12, 10/12 → order: 8/12, 9/12, 10/12 → 2/3 < 3/4 < 5/6

### Fractions of amounts
1. Divide the amount by the denominator (find 1 part)
2. Multiply by the numerator

**Example:** 3/5 of 40 = (40 ÷ 5) × 3 = 8 × 3 = 24

### Fractions in ratio problems (R8/N11)
"In a ratio of 3:5, what fraction of the total is the first share?"
- Total parts = 3 + 5 = 8
- First share = 3/8 of the total

## Likely misconceptions (from mark schemes and research)

- **MISCONCEPTION:** Simplifying 15/20 by dividing by 5 → 3/4 is correct, but students sometimes divide numerator only: 3/20 (forgot to divide denominator too). Correction: BOTH numerator and denominator must be divided by the same number.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** "Give 1/4 of Andrew's share" interpreted as "give £1.40" (reading 1/4 as 1.4). Correction: 1/4 means one part out of four; divide by 4, not by 10.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Ordering fractions by comparing numerators without common denominators: "3/4 > 5/6 because 5 > 3." Correction: must convert to common denominator first (3/4 = 9/12; 5/6 = 10/12; 10/12 > 9/12 so 5/6 > 3/4).
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Mixed number ÷ by adding numerators: 2⅓ ÷ 4/5 → student writes 3/5. Correction: must convert to improper fraction first (this is covered in Ch9 for division; in Ch8 the key skill is the conversion itself).
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Writing "3 out of 5" as a probability/ratio answer instead of 3/5. AQA mark scheme states probabilities must be written as fractions, decimals or percentages — not as "3 out of 5".

## Exam angles (from AQA past papers)

**How N8/N10/R8 appears in Paper 1 (non-calculator):**

1. **Simplify a fraction (1 mark):** "Simplify 24/36" — HCF(24,36) = 12 → 2/3
2. **Fraction of an amount (2 marks):** "Find 3/8 of 56" — (56 ÷ 8) × 3 = 21; M1 for dividing by 8; A1 for 21
3. **Mixed number/improper conversion (1 mark):** "Write 2¾ as an improper fraction" — (2×4)+3 = 11/4
4. **Ordering fractions (2 marks):** "Write these in order of size, smallest first: 2/3, 5/9, 7/12" — common denominator 36: 24/36, 20/36, 21/36 → 5/9 < 7/12 < 2/3
5. **Ratio fraction (2 marks):** "Andrew and Bruce share money in ratio 5:6. What fraction of the total does Bruce receive?" — total parts = 11; Bruce's fraction = 6/11
6. **Improper → mixed (1 mark):** "Write 23/5 as a mixed number" — 23 ÷ 5 = 4 remainder 3 → 4⅗
7. **Fraction of amount in context (3 marks):** "A recipe uses 3/4 of a cup of flour for 6 biscuits. How much flour for 10 biscuits?" — links to proportion (Ch9 preview)

**AQA wording patterns:**
- "Give your answer as a fraction in its simplest form"
- "Write [improper fraction] as a mixed number" / "Write [mixed number] as an improper fraction"
- "What fraction of the total is…"

**Mark scheme patterns:**
- Simplest form is explicitly required; 6/8 instead of 3/4 loses the A1
- "oe fraction" = mark can be awarded for equivalent unsimplified fractions at intermediate steps; final answer must be simplified when asked
- ft applies: if student has a wrong fraction, subsequent calculation using that fraction can still score M marks
