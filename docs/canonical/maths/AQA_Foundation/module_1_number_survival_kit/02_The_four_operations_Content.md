## Key concepts and vocabulary

- **Addition:** combining two or more quantities; the result is a sum
- **Subtraction:** finding the difference between two quantities
- **Multiplication:** repeated addition; scaling; the result is a product
- **Division:** sharing equally or finding how many times a number fits; the result is a quotient
- **Sum:** the result of an addition
- **Difference:** the result of a subtraction (always positive)
- **Product:** the result of a multiplication
- **Quotient:** the result of a division
- **Remainder:** the amount left over after an integer division
- **Inverse operation:** the operation that reverses another: subtraction reverses addition; division reverses multiplication
- **Column method:** a formal written method where digits are aligned by place value column
- **Carry:** when a column sum exceeds 9, the tens digit is carried to the next column
- **Borrow:** in subtraction, taking value from the next column when the current column is insufficient
- **Profit:** selling price minus cost price (positive = profit)
- **Loss:** cost price minus selling price (when selling price is lower)
- **Balance:** the net amount in an account (can be negative if overdrawn)
- **Debit:** money going out; credit: money coming in

## Required methods

### Addition and subtraction (column method)
1. Write numbers in columns, aligning digits by place value (decimal points must align for decimals)
2. Start from the rightmost column; work left
3. Carry (addition) or borrow (subtraction) when needed
4. For subtraction, always check: answer + smaller number = larger number (inverse check)

### Multiplication (formal column)
1. For integers: multiply each digit of the bottom number by each digit of the top number in turn; shift one column left for tens digit
2. Add partial products
3. For decimals: ignore the decimal point, multiply as integers, then count total decimal places across both numbers and reinsert point in answer

### Division (short division / long division)
1. Short division: work left to right; carry remainders as you go
2. Long division: use for multi-digit divisors; estimate, multiply, subtract, bring down
3. Decimal answers: continue past the decimal point rather than leaving a remainder in context questions

### Choosing a method
- Mental: simple numbers, multiples of 10/100, near-doubles
- Written: multi-digit numbers; decimals; any question on Paper 1 (non-calculator)
- Calculator: Paper 2 and 3 only — but must still show intention/method

## Likely misconceptions (from mark schemes and research)

- **MISCONCEPTION:** Misaligning decimal points in column addition/subtraction (e.g. adding 3.4 + 0.06 by aligning the rightmost digits rather than the decimal points). Correction: always draw a vertical line for the decimal point first.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Forgetting to carry in column multiplication (e.g. 47 × 8: 7 × 8 = 56, write 6, carry 5; then 4 × 8 = 32 + 5 = 37 — students often forget the carry). Correction: write the carry digit clearly above the next column.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** "Multiplying a decimal by 10 just adds a zero." Correction: multiplying by 10 shifts every digit one place to the left. Adding a zero only works for integers. 0.6 × 10 = 6, not 0.60.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** "Division always makes a number smaller." Correction: dividing by a fraction or decimal less than 1 makes the number larger (e.g. 6 ÷ 0.5 = 12).
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Treating division as commutative: 8 ÷ 4 = 4 ÷ 8. Correction: division is not commutative — order matters.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Writing answer to £ problems without rounding to 2 d.p. (e.g. £1.2 instead of £1.20). Correction: money answers should be given in pounds and pence (2 d.p.) unless asked otherwise.

## Exam angles (from AQA past papers)

**How N2/N3 appears in Paper 1 (non-calculator):**

1. **Multi-step cost problem (3–4 marks):** "400g of cereal costs 49p per 100g. 250g of pasta costs 14p per 100g. Find the total cost in £." Tests multiplication, addition, unit conversion. Mark scheme: M1 for attempt to find cost of cereal (or pasta), A1 for each correct sub-total, A1 for correct total in £.
2. **Inverse check question (1–2 marks):** "Work out the missing number: □ × 14 = 364" — tests division as inverse of multiplication
3. **Decimal calculation (2–3 marks):** "Work out 3.7 × 4" or "Work out 8.4 ÷ 0.4" — non-calculator; formal method expected
4. **Finance context (3–4 marks):** "A shop buys an item for £18. They sell it for £23.50. What is the profit? What is the percentage profit?" — tests N2 in context; percentage profit may also draw on R9 (later chapter)
5. **'Show that' question (2 marks):** "Show that 13 × 14 = 182" — student must write the calculation method, not just state the answer; answer-only = 0 marks

**AQA wording patterns:**
- "Work out" — standard instruction; written method expected on Paper 1
- "Show that" — working is mandatory; answer alone scores 0
- "Give your answer in £" — unit conversion may be needed
- "Without a calculator, work out" — explicit reminder that Paper 1 is non-calculator

**Mark scheme notes:**
- M1 awarded for a correct method, even with arithmetic error
- A1 awarded only for correct accuracy following correct method
- "oe" (or equivalent): alternative valid methods score equally
