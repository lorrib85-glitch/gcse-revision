## Key concepts and vocabulary

- **Index (plural: indices):** the power to which a base is raised; in 5Ві, the index is 3, the base is 5
- **Square:** a number raised to the power 2; e.g. 6ВІ = 36; "36 is a perfect square"
- **Cube:** a number raised to the power 3; e.g. 4Ві = 64; "64 is a perfect cube"
- **Square root (вҲҡ):** the inverse of squaring; вҲҡ36 = 6 (positive root only in GCSE unless stated otherwise)
- **Cube root (вҲӣ):** the inverse of cubing; вҲӣ64 = 4
- **Perfect squares (must know up to 15ВІ):** 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, 121, 144, 169, 196, 225
- **Perfect cubes (must know up to 10Ві):** 1, 8, 27, 64, 125, 216, 343, 512, 729, 1000
- **Standard form (scientific notation):** A Г— 10вҒҝ where 1 вүӨ A < 10 and n is an integer
  - Large number: n positive; e.g. 149,600,000 = 1.496 Г— 10вҒё
  - Small number: n negative; e.g. 0.000037 = 3.7 Г— 10вҒ»вҒө
- **Standard form on calculator display:** "1.496 E 8" means 1.496 Г— 10вҒё; recognise this notation
- **Reciprocal:** the reciprocal of x is 1/x; e.g. reciprocal of 4 = 0.25

## Required methods

### Calculating powers
- xВІ = x Г— x
- xВі = x Г— x Г— x
- Powers of 10: 10вҒҝ = 1 followed by n zeros; e.g. 10вҒө = 100,000

### Index laws (for positive integer indices)
- aбөҗ Г— aвҒҝ = aбөҗвҒәвҒҝ (multiply: add indices)
- aбөҗ Г· aвҒҝ = aбөҗвҒ»вҒҝ (divide: subtract indices)
- (aбөҗ)вҒҝ = aбөҗЛЈвҒҝ (power of a power: multiply indices)

**Note:** index laws apply only when bases are identical. 2Ві Г— 3ВІ вү  6вҒө.

### Converting to standard form
1. Write as A Г— 10вҒҝ where 1 вүӨ A < 10
2. Count how many places the decimal point moves to get from A to the original number
3. If original is large (decimal moves right to get back): n is positive
4. If original is small (decimal moves left to get back): n is negative

**Examples:**
- 8,300,000 = 8.3 Г— 10вҒ¶ (decimal moves 6 places right)
- 0.000037 = 3.7 Г— 10вҒ»вҒө (decimal moves 5 places left)

### Converting from standard form
1. Multiply A by 10вҒҝ
2. If n positive: move decimal point n places right (add zeros if needed)
3. If n negative: move decimal point n places left (add leading zeros if needed)

### Standard form arithmetic (without calculator вҖ” simple cases)
- (A Г— 10бөҗ) Г— (B Г— 10вҒҝ) = (A Г— B) Г— 10бөҗвҒәвҒҝ вҶ’ check if result needs adjustment to keep 1 вүӨ A < 10
- (A Г— 10бөҗ) Г· (B Г— 10вҒҝ) = (A Г· B) Г— 10бөҗвҒ»вҒҝ вҶ’ check if adjustment needed
- **Example:** (3 Г— 10вҒҙ) Г— (4 Г— 10Ві) = 12 Г— 10вҒ· = 1.2 Г— 10вҒё

### Interpreting calculator display
- "1.23 E 5" = 1.23 Г— 10вҒө = 123,000
- "6.5 E -3" = 6.5 Г— 10вҒ»Ві = 0.0065

## Likely misconceptions (from mark schemes and research)

- **MISCONCEPTION:** "8 Г— 10вҒ· is the answer when the calculation requires 2 Г— 10ВІ." (Losing track of base-10 arithmetic when subtracting indices) вҖ” from Series Content. Correction: subtract indices carefully; check that the A part satisfies 1 вүӨ A < 10 after calculation.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** 80,000,000 Г· 200 in standard form: students write 4 Г— 10вҒ¶ (off by a power of 10). Correction: 80,000,000 Г· 200 = 400,000 = 4 Г— 10вҒө; or in standard form: (8 Г— 10вҒ·) Г· (2 Г— 10ВІ) = 4 Г— 10вҒө.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** "Standard form 12 Г— 10вҒҙ" вҖ” the A part must be вүҘ 1 and < 10; 12 Г— 10вҒҙ is not standard form; it must be written as 1.2 Г— 10вҒө.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** вҲҡ(a + b) = вҲҡa + вҲҡb (for example, вҲҡ(16+9) = вҲҡ16 + вҲҡ9 = 7, not 5). Correction: evaluate inside the root first; вҲҡ(16+9) = вҲҡ25 = 5.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Negative index means the number is negative. Correction: A Г— 10вҒ»вҒҝ is a very small positive number, not negative (e.g. 3.7 Г— 10вҒ»вҒө = 0.000037 > 0).

## Exam angles (from AQA past papers)

**How N6/N7/N9 appears in Paper 1 (non-calculator):**

1. **Recall of perfect square/cube (1 mark):** "Work out вҲӣ27" = 3; or "Work out вҲҡ144" = 12
2. **Standard form conversion (1вҖ“2 marks):** "Write 80,000,000 Г· 200 in standard form." вҖ” M1 for working; A1 for 4 Г— 10вҒө (not 4 Г— 10вҒ¶)
3. **Index law application (2вҖ“3 marks):** "Simplify 8 Г— 2вҒ¶ Г— 2вҒҙ. Give answer as a power of 2." вҖ” 8 = 2Ві; 2Ві Г— 2вҒ¶ Г— 2вҒҙ = 2В№Ві

6. **Calculator display interpretation (1 mark):** "A calculator shows 3.82 E 6. Write this as an ordinary number." вҖ” 3,820,000
7. **"Give answer in standard form" (1 mark follow-on):** appears after a calculation; most common error вҖ” forgetting to adjust A to be between 1 and 10

**AQA wording patterns:**
- "Write in standard form"
- "Give your answer as a power of [base]"
- "Write as an ordinary number"
- "Give your answer correct to [n] significant figures" (often follows standard form questions)
