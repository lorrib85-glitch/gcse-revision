# Chapter 10: Decimals, fractions and percentages — Content

## Series: AQA GCSE Maths Foundation — Module 1: Number survival kit

## Big question

Why does the examiner sometimes ask for "35%" and sometimes for "7/20" and sometimes for "0.35" — and how do you know when to convert and which form gives the cleaner answer?

---

## AQA syllabus coverage

**Spec codes: N10, N12, R9**

- Interconversion: fraction ↔ decimal ↔ percentage (N10)
- Key equivalents: must know without calculation: 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%; 1/4 = 0.25 = 25%; 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%; 1/5 = 0.2 = 20%; 2/5 = 0.4 = 40%; 3/5 = 0.6 = 60%; 4/5 = 0.8 = 80%; 1/10 = 0.1 = 10%; 1/100 = 0.01 = 1%; 1/3 ≈ 0.333 = 33⅓%; 1/8 = 0.125 = 12.5%
- Ordering mixed FDP values (N10)
- Converting a fraction to a decimal (N10): divide numerator by denominator
- Converting a decimal to a fraction (N10): place value — e.g. 0.35 = 35/100 = 7/20
- Converting a percentage to a decimal: divide by 100 (move decimal left 2 places)
- Converting a decimal to a percentage: multiply by 100 (move decimal right 2 places)
- Converting a percentage to a fraction: write as /100 and simplify
- Interpreting percentages as operators: e.g. 35% of 240 = 0.35 × 240 (N12)
- Comparing two quantities using percentages (R9)
- Expressing one quantity as a percentage of another (R9)

## Key concepts and vocabulary

- **FDP:** shorthand for Fractions, Decimals, Percentages — all three represent part of a whole
- **Per cent:** "per hundred" — 35% means 35 out of every 100 parts; x% = x/100 = x ÷ 100
- **Multiplier:** the decimal equivalent of a percentage used in calculations; 35% → multiplier = 0.35; 120% → multiplier = 1.2
- **Terminating decimal:** a decimal that ends after a finite number of digits; 3/8 = 0.375 (terminates)
- **Recurring decimal:** a decimal where a digit or group of digits repeats forever; 1/3 = 0.333…; converting recurring decimals to fractions is Higher only
- **Percentage of an amount:** e.g. 35% of 240 = 0.35 × 240 = 84; or (35/100) × 240 = 84
- **Percentage comparison:** express both quantities as fractions of the same whole, then compare

## Required methods

### Fraction → Decimal
Divide numerator by denominator:
- 3/8 → 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375 (on Paper 1: use bus-stop division)
- 7/4 → 7 ÷ 4 = 1.75

### Decimal → Fraction (terminating decimals only — Foundation)
Use place value:
- 0.35 → 35/100 = 7/20 (simplify using HCF = 5)
- 0.625 → 625/1000 = 5/8 (HCF = 125)
- 0.3 → 3/10 (already in simplest form)

### Percentage → Decimal → Fraction
- Percentage → decimal: divide by 100 (or move decimal left 2 places): 45% → 0.45
- Decimal → fraction: use place value: 0.45 → 45/100 = 9/20

### Ordering FDP values
Convert all to decimal form, then compare:
- 45%, 9/20, 0.44: 0.45, 0.45, 0.44 → order: 0.44, then 9/20 = 45% (tied)

### Percentage of an amount (N12)
Method: percentage × amount using multiplier (decimal):
- 35% of 240 = 0.35 × 240 = 84
- Alternatively: 10% = 24; 35% = 3 × 24 + 5% (=12): 72 + 12 = 84 (build-up method)

### Express as a percentage of another quantity (R9)
Divide the part by the whole, then multiply by 100:
- 54 out of 80 as a percentage = (54/80) × 100 = 67.5%

## Likely misconceptions (from mark schemes and research)

- **MISCONCEPTION:** "Percentage of a quantity: 40% of 80 = 40 × 80 = 3200." Correction: 40% = 0.40; 0.40 × 80 = 32. OR: 10% of 80 = 8; 40% = 4 × 8 = 32.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Confusing 35% and 3.5 (not 0.35). Correction: 35% = 35/100 = 0.35; divide by 100, not 10.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** "0.4 = 4%" (dividing by 10 instead of multiplying by 100). Correction: decimal → percentage = ×100; 0.4 × 100 = 40%.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** Ordering 1/3, 0.3, 30%: student writes 0.3 = 30% = 3/10 and 1/3 > 30% — correct, but some students write 1/3 = 30% (confusing with 3/10). Correction: 1/3 ≈ 0.333 = 33.3%; 0.3 = 30%; 1/3 > 0.3.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** When converting 0.625 to a fraction: writing 625/100 instead of 625/1000 (miscounting decimal places). Correction: 3 decimal places = denominator of 1000.
- **MISCONCEPTION:** "35 and 20% are equivalent" if comparing 35 out of 50 vs 20% as a decimal. Correction: 35 out of 50 = 70%, which is greater than 20%.

## Exam angles (from AQA past papers)

**How N10/N12/R9 appears in Paper 1 (non-calculator):**

1. **Convert fraction to percentage (1–2 marks):** "Write 13/20 as a percentage" — 13/20 × 100 = 65%
2. **Ordering FDP (2 marks):** "Write in order, smallest first: 3/5, 0.65, 55%" — convert: 0.6, 0.65, 0.55 → 55%, 3/5, 0.65
3. **Percentage of amount (2 marks):** "Find 35% of 240" — multiplier: 0.35 × 240 = 84; or build-up method
4. **Express as percentage (2 marks):** "Express 27 as a percentage of 90." — (27/90) × 100 = 30%
5. **FDP comparison in context (3 marks):** "Shona has 14 dresses. 50% are red. She gives 5 to charity and buys 1 new red. What percentage are now red?" — (7+1)/(14−5+1) = 8/10 = 80%
6. **Which is greater? (2 marks):** "Which is greater: 2/5 or 0.45? Show your working." — 2/5 = 0.40; 0.45 > 0.40 so 0.45 is greater

**AQA wording patterns:**
- "Write [fraction/decimal/percentage] as a [fraction/decimal/percentage]"
- "Which is larger? You must show working."
- "Express [A] as a percentage of [B]"
- "Find [n]% of [amount]"

**Mark scheme patterns:**
- Key equivalents questions: B1 for correct conversion; method mark not needed for simple cases
- Percentage of amount: M1 for correct multiplier method (0.35 × 240 seen) even if arithmetic wrong; A1 for 84
- "oe" — any valid equivalent form (fraction, decimal, percentage all accepted unless specific form required)
- Context problems with % update: must recalculate total (e.g. "buys 1 new" changes total count)
