# Episode 3: What is the family for? — Content

## Series: Families and households (AQA 8192, Paper 1 Section A)

---

## Big question

Does the family exist to serve society — or to serve certain groups within it?

---

## Syllabus coverage

- Functionalist perspective: family as essential to social order
- Murdock's four functions of the nuclear family: sexual, reproductive, economic, educational (socialisation)
- Parsons' two key functions: primary socialisation of children + stabilisation of adult personalities
- Instrumental role (male, breadwinner, disciplinarian) and expressive role (female, emotional/caring, childcare)
- Evaluation of functionalism: ignores conflict, out of date (women now work), ignores family diversity
- Marxist perspective (Engels): isolated nuclear family enables men to confirm paternity and ensure wealth stays within the family through private inheritance → maintains capitalism
- New Right perspective (Charles Murray): nuclear family encourages self-sufficiency, reduces welfare expenditure, passes on shared moral values — lone-parent families produce an "underclass"
- Evaluation of New Right: exaggerates decline of the nuclear family; most lone parents want to work

---

## Key concepts / vocabulary

- **Functionalism:** society works as a system where each part (including the family) performs essential functions
- **Primary socialisation:** learning norms and values in early childhood, usually within the family
- **Stabilisation of adult personalities:** the family provides emotional support and stability for adults (Parsons' "warm bath" theory)
- **Instrumental role:** rational, breadwinner, disciplinarian function (typically male — Parsons)
- **Expressive role:** emotional, caring, domestic function (typically female — Parsons)
- **New Right:** conservative perspective; supports the nuclear family; critical of lone-parent and welfare-dependent families
- **Underclass (Murray):** group at the bottom of society characterised by dependency on welfare

---

## Named evidence

- **George Murdock** (functionalist): four functions of the nuclear family — sexual (regulate sexual behaviour), reproductive (produce next generation), economic (provide food/shelter/resources), educational (socialise children into norms and values). Source: *Social Structure*, 1949.
- **Talcott Parsons** (functionalist): two irreducible functions — primary socialisation + stabilisation of adult personalities. Instrumental + expressive role distinction. Source: 'The social structure of the family', *The Family: Its Functions and Destiny*, 1959.
- **Friedrich Engels** (Marxist): the isolated nuclear family enables private property inheritance; men need to know paternity to pass on wealth → family protects capitalism. Source: *The Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State*, 1884.
- **Charles Murray** (New Right): nuclear family best for self-reliance and moral values; lone-parent families produce welfare dependence and an underclass. Source: *Losing Ground*, 1984.

---

## Likely misconceptions

- MISCONCEPTION: "Parsons and Murdock say the same thing." Correction: Murdock identifies four functions; Parsons narrows to two irreducible functions and adds the role distinction.
- MISCONCEPTION: "New Right and functionalism are the same." Correction: New Right is politically motivated and criticises non-nuclear family forms; functionalists describe what the family does, not what form it should take.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Marxists and functionalists disagree about whether the family works — but they agree on who it benefits." Correction: functionalists say the family benefits everyone; Marxists say it primarily benefits capitalism/the ruling class.
