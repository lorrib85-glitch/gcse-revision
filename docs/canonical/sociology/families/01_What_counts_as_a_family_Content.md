# Episode 1: What counts as a family? — Content

## Series: Families and households (AQA 8192, Paper 1 Section A)

---

## Big question

Is a family defined by biology, law, love, or living together?

---

## Syllabus coverage

- Definitions: family vs household (precise distinction)
- Family households and non-family households
- Nuclear family (two-generation: married/cohabiting parents + dependent children)
- Extended family (relatives beyond the nuclear family, often three generations)
- Modified extended family (relatives living apart but maintaining regular contact and support)
- Lone-parent (single-parent) family
- Reconstituted (blended/step) family
- Same-sex family
- Beanpole family (multigenerational, few members per generation)
- Cohabitation
- Civil partnership
- Living Apart Together (LAT) couples
- Dependent children
- Life-course diversity — different household and family forms across one person's life

## Key concepts / vocabulary

- **Family:** people linked by birth, marriage, civil partnership, adoption or cohabitation in long-term relationships
- **Household:** one person living alone, or a group of people living at the same address and sharing facilities such as a kitchen, bathroom or living room — not always a family
- **Family household:** a household containing people connected as a family
- **Non-family household:** a household containing one person living alone or unrelated people sharing, such as friends or students
- **Nuclear family:** two generations living together, usually parent(s) and dependent child(ren)
- **Extended family:** family including relatives beyond the nuclear family, such as grandparents, aunts, uncles or cousins
- **Modified extended family:** extended family members who do not all live together but keep regular contact and provide support
- **Lone-parent family:** one parent living with dependent child(ren)
- **Reconstituted family:** family where one or both partners have children from previous relationships
- **Same-sex family:** family headed by a same-sex couple, with or without dependent children
- **Beanpole family:** multigenerational family with few members in each generation, linked to declining birth rates and increased life expectancy
- **Dependent children:** children who rely on adults for care and financial support
- **Cohabitation:** living with a partner outside of marriage
- **Civil partnership:** legally recognised partnership that gives a couple similar legal rights to marriage
- **LAT (Living Apart Together):** committed couple living at separate addresses
- **Life course:** culturally defined sequence of age categories from birth to death; family and household forms can change across a person's life
- **Kinship:** family relationships based on blood, marriage, adoption or long-term social ties

## Likely misconceptions

- MISCONCEPTION: "A household is always a family." Correction: a household can be one person alone or unrelated people sharing.
- MISCONCEPTION: "A family always lives in one household." Correction: some family members live apart but still maintain regular contact and support, for example in modified extended families.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Extended families always live together." Correction: modified extended families live apart but maintain regular contact and support.
- MISCONCEPTION: "A nuclear family always means a married mum, dad and children." Correction: the course definition allows married or cohabiting parents; the key idea is a two-generation family with dependent children.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Same-sex couples can't have children." Correction: same-sex families include couples with children through adoption, fertility treatment, surrogacy or previous relationships.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Civil partnership and cohabitation are the same." Correction: cohabitation means living together without marriage; civil partnership is a legally recognised relationship status.
