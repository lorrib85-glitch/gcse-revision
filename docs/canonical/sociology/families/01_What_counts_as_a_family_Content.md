# Episode 1: What counts as a family? — Content

## Series: Families and households (AQA 8192, Paper 1 Section A)

---

## Big question

Is a family defined by biology, law, love, or living together?

---

## Syllabus coverage

- Definitions: family vs household (precise distinction)
- Nuclear family (two-generation: married/cohabiting parents + children)
- Extended family (classic three-generation vs modified extended)
- Lone-parent (single-parent) family
- Reconstituted (blended/step) family
- Same-sex family
- Beanpole family (multigenerational, few members per generation)
- Cohabitation
- Civil partnership
- Living Apart Together (LAT) couples
- Life-course diversity — different household types across one person's life

---

## Key concepts / vocabulary

- **Family:** people linked by birth, marriage, civil partnership, adoption or cohabitation in long-term relationships
- **Household:** one person living alone, or a group sharing an address and facilities — not always a family
- **Family household vs non-family household**
- **Life course:** culturally defined sequence of age categories from birth to death
- **Dependent children**
- **Cohabitation:** living with a partner outside of marriage
- **LAT (Living Apart Together):** committed couple living at separate addresses
- **Beanpole family:** multigenerational, few members per generation (result of declining birth rate + increased life expectancy)
- **Reconstituted family:** family where one or both partners have children from previous relationships
- **Civil partnership:** legally recognised same-sex (and now opposite-sex) couple relationship

---

## Likely misconceptions

- MISCONCEPTION: "A household is always a family." Correction: a household can be one person alone or unrelated people sharing.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Extended families always live together." Correction: modified extended families live apart but maintain regular contact and support.
- MISCONCEPTION: "Same-sex couples can't have children." Correction: same-sex families include couples with children through adoption, IVF or previous relationships.
