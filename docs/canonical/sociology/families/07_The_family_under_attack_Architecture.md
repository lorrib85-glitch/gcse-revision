## 6. Build notes

This episode should feel more serious. Sociology does not just ask what families are for — it asks who may be harmed or exploited within them.

Keep Zaretsky and Delphy/Leonard as the two core named sociologist anchors. Do not let Engels or generic radical feminism take over the episode. Engels can be background knowledge if needed, but the canonical build should prioritise the named researchers most useful for AQA family answers.

The dark side section needs careful handling: present evidence factually, avoid sensationalism, and note limitations of official statistics. The aim is academic precision and safeguarding-sensitive language, not shock value.

The strongest exam answer compares perspectives: Functionalists see support and stability; Marxists see capitalism; Feminists see patriarchy; difference feminists warn against overgeneralising women's experiences.
