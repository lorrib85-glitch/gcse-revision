## 2. Stage mapping (7-stage rhythm)

### Stage 1 — Hook & prior recall
- **Proposed:** "Who decides what a normal family is?" Retrieve Episode 1 family types. Set up the question: are all these types treated equally?
- **Components:** `ChapterHookScreen`, `PriorKnowledgeRecall`

### Stage 2 — Core concept decode
- **Proposed:** Precise definition of the conventional family (Oakley). One concept at a time — do not mix in gender roles yet.
- **Components:** `ConceptReveal`, `FillInTheBlanksBlock`

### Stage 3 — Sociologist evidence
- **Proposed:** Oakley profile card — perspective (feminist), key claim, date, source. Students must be able to write "Oakley argues that…" correctly.
- **Components:** `KeyFigureReveal`

### Stage 4 — Challenge & perspectives
- **Proposed:** Challenge the conventional family idea: is it a description or a judgment? Show that the conventional family treats other forms as abnormal.
- **Components:** `MisconceptionCheck` (myth vs reality cards)

### Stage 5 — Apply & scenario
- **Proposed:** Scenario check: which family is being treated as "normal" in this situation? Students identify the conventional family assumption being made.
- **Components:** `ColSortBlock` or scenario cards

### Stage 6 — Exam build
- **Proposed:** 3-mark describe (describe the conventional family) and 4-mark apply (apply Oakley's argument to a short scenario).
- **Components:** `GuidedExamResponse`, `FaceTheExaminer`

### Stage 7 — Quick fire retrieval
- **Proposed:** Five questions: define conventional family, name Oakley's perspective, state one thing Oakley links the conventional family to, define social construct, give one example of a family type that challenges the conventional ideal.
- **Components:** `QuickRecallScreen`

## 3. Active learning interactions

- `MisconceptionCheck`: myth vs reality cards ("The nuclear family is natural" / "Most children grow up in nuclear families")
- `KeyFigureReveal`: Oakley profile card — perspective, key claim, best used for
- `FillInTheBlanksBlock` or quote builder: "Oakley argues that…"
- Scenario check: which family is being treated as "normal" in this situation?

## 4. Retrieval points

- Oakley's definition of the conventional family (verbatim-level accuracy)
- Oakley's perspective (feminist) and her key claim
- One way the conventional family idea relates to gender inequality
- One way family diversity challenges the conventional family idea

## 5. Exam skill focus

- 3-mark describe: describe the conventional family
- 4-mark apply: apply Oakley's argument to a short scenario

## 6. Build notes

Open with "who decides what a normal family is?" — teenagers already feel judged on this. The sociological move is to show that "normal" is always political.
