## 2. Stage mapping (7-stage rhythm)

### Stage 1 — Hook & prior recall
- **Proposed:** "Are you more independent than your parents were at your age?" Connect to teenager's lived experience. Retrieve Episode 4 beanpole family and sandwich generation concepts.
- **Components:** `ChapterHookScreen`, `PriorKnowledgeRecall`

### Stage 2 — Core concept decode
- **Proposed:** Childhood as a social construct. March of Progress view vs conflict view — two frameworks for understanding change.
- **Components:** `ConceptReveal`, `VisualLearning`

### Stage 3 — Sociologist evidence
- **Proposed:** Aries (social construct), Postman (disappearance), Jenks (changing not disappearing). Three researchers with distinct positions.
- **Components:** `KeyFigureReveal`, `TheoryCompareBlock`

### Stage 4 — Challenge & perspectives
- **Proposed:** Challenge the March of Progress view: conflict view — class, gender, ethnicity produce unequal childhoods. Palmer: toxic childhood adds a different critical angle.
- **Components:** `MisconceptionCheck`, `TheoryCompareBlock`

- **Components:** `VisualLearning`, `TimelineChain`

### Stage 6 — Exam build
- **Proposed:** 4-mark explain (explain one way parent-child relationships have changed). 12-mark preparation: "Discuss how far sociologists would agree that childhood and parent-child relationships have improved over time."
- **Components:** `GuidedExamResponse`, `FaceTheExaminer`

- **Proposed:** Define childhood as a social construct + one piece of evidence, March of Progress — two improvements, conflict view — one inequality, Postman's key claim, Jenks' alternative, definitions of boomerang children and sandwich generation.

## 4. Retrieval points

- Definition of childhood as a social construct with one piece of evidence (historical or cultural)
- March of Progress view — two improvements to childhood
- Conflict view — one inequality that shapes childhood (class, gender or ethnicity)
- Postman's key claim about childhood
- Jenks' alternative argument
- Definition of boomerang children and sandwich generation
- Link between increased life expectancy, ageing population and beanpole family structure

## 5. Exam skill focus

- 4-mark explain: explain one way parent-child relationships have changed
- 12-mark preparation: "Discuss how far sociologists would agree that childhood and parent-child relationships have improved over time"

## 6. Build notes

This connects directly to teenage life: independence, parents, rules, money, school, leaving home and returning home. The demographic context (life expectancy, beanpole families, sandwich generation) should be woven in naturally — it explains why family relationships across generations have become more complex, not just why childhood has changed.
